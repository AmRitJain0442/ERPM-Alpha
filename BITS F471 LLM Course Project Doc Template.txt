PROJECT WORK DOCUMENTATION
LLM Evaluation Research: USD/INR Exchange Rate Prediction
1. Project Overview
1.1 Project Title
Predicting USD/INR Exchange Rate Volatility Using News Sentiment Analysis and Multi-Persona LLM Agents
1.2 Objective
The primary objective of this research is to evaluate the capability of Large Language Models to predict USD/INR exchange rate movements and volatility when augmented with real-time global news data (GDELT). We aim to assess whether a "multi-persona" LLM system—simulating a trading floor with specialized agents—can outperform traditional statistical models (GARCH, ARIMA) and machine learning baselines (XGBoost).
1.3 Research Questions
1. Can LLM agents initialized with specific financial personas (e.g., Macro Analyst, Risk Manager) provide more accurate market forecasts than generic prompts?
2. Does the integration of GDELT news sentiment data with historical price data improve the predictive accuracy of LLMs compared to using price data alone?
3. How effectively can LLMs identify high-volatility "danger days" based on geopolitical and economic news signals?
4. Does an ensemble of LLM agents and statistical models outperform either approach individually?

2. Literature Review & Background
2.1 Related Work
Financial forecasting has traditionally relied on econometric models like ARIMA and GARCH. More recently, Deep Learning models (LSTMs, Transformers) have been applied to time-series data. However, the application of LLMs for direct numerical forecasting based on textual news analysis is a nascent field. Existing work often focuses on simple sentiment analysis (positive/negative), whereas this project leverages GDELT's rich metadata (Goldstein Scale, AvgTone) and LLM reasoning.
2.2 Research Gaps & Motivation
Most current LLM evaluations in finance focus on developed markets (US/EU) using text classification or summarization. There is a distinct lack of research applying advanced LLM agents to emerging markets like India, which are characterized by higher volatility and stronger sensitivity to news sentiment. Our project addresses this gap by evaluating LLMs as *agents* simulating decision-making processes in the specific context of the Indian Rupee (INR), where geopolitical and domestic news often drive rapid market shifts that traditional models miss.
2.3 Background on Task/Domain
The USD/INR currency pair is uniquely complex, influenced by a mix of global factors (US Fed policy, oil prices) and highly specific local drivers (RBI intervention, Indian elections, budget announcements). Unlike stable developed market currencies, the INR often exhibits sharp, sentiment-driven volatility spikes. This domain requires processing vast amounts of unstructured text (news) and understanding the nuances of the Indian economy, a task well-suited for multimodal LLM workflows that can parse "Fedspeak" alongside local political developments.

4. Evaluation Methodology
4.1 Evaluation Framework
We implemented a custom "Multi-Persona Market Simulation" framework. The system simulates a trading floor with 9 specialized AI agents (personas).
- **Architecture:** The framework ingests daily GDELT news and market data.
- **Process:** Each persona analyzes the data from its unique perspective (e.g., a "Technical Analyst" looks at price trends, a "Political Analyst" looks at election news) and outputs a forecast and confidence score.
- **Aggregation:** Responses are aggregated using a weighted ensemble approach to produce a final prediction.
4.2 Evaluation Metrics
We use standard regression and classification metrics to evaluate performance:
- **RMSE (Root Mean Square Error):** To measure the magnitude of prediction errors.
- **MAE (Mean Absolute Error):** To measure average error magnitude.
- **Directional Accuracy:** The percentage of days the model correctly predicted the direction of the move (Up/Down).
- **Volatility Classification Accuracy:** Precision/Recall in identifying high-volatility days.
4.3 Prompting Strategy
We utilized a **Persona-Based Chain-of-Thought** strategy.
- **System Prompts:** defined specific roles (e.g., "You are a veteran Macro Analyst...").
- **Context:** Prompts included a summary of the previous day's news, current technical indicators (RSI, Bollinger Bands), and recent price history.
- **Task:** Agents were asked to "think step-by-step" about the impact of news on the exchange rate before providing a numerical forecast.

4.4 Sampling Parameters
- **Temperature:** Set low (0.1 - 0.3) to encourage deterministic and analytical outputs suitable for financial forecasting.
- **Top_p/Top_k:** Standard settings to ensure focused generation.

7. Results & Analysis
7.1 Quantitative Results
*Note: Specific numerical results are available in `simulation_summary.csv` and `model_results_comprehensive.txt`.*
- **Directional Accuracy:** The multi-persona LLM system achieved competitive directional accuracy compared to random chance (50%).
- **Volatility Prediction:** The integration of GDELT news significantly improved the model's ability to foresee high-volatility days (9-12 day lead time for political signals).
7.2 Qualitative Analysis
- **Strengths:** The LLM excelled at interpreting complex geopolitical events (e.g., elections, trade deals) that pure statistical models missed.
- **Weaknesses:** The LLM occasionally "hallucinated" strong market reactions to minor news events or struggled with precise numerical targets compared to GARCH.
7.3 Visualizations
Key visualizations include:
- **News Volume vs. Volatility:** Showing the correlation between news intensity and market movement.
- **Persona Agreement:** Charts showing disjoints between "Technical" and "Fundamental" personas during market shocks.

8. Discussion & Insights
8.1 Key Findings
- **News is a Volatility Predictor:** While predicting exact price levels is hard, news sentiment is a strong leading indicator for volatility.
- **Persona Diversity Matters:** The "Risk Manager" and "Macro Analyst" personas often diverged, providing a more robust ensemble prediction than a single "Forecaster" prompt.
- **Debiasing is Critical:** Early iterations (V3) suffered from bullish/bearish biases. The V4 "debiased" architecture with randomized labels significantly improved reliability.
8.2 Practical Implications
For practitioners, this system suggests that LLMs are best used as "Copilots" for risk assessment—flagging potential danger days based on news—rather than as standalone trading bots.
8.3 Limitations
- **Cost/Latency:** Querying an LLM 9 times daily (for each persona) is slower and more expensive than running a regression model.
- **Context Limit:** While Gemini has a large context, we still had to summarize daily news, potentially losing granular details.

9. Next Steps & Future Work
9.1 Future Research Directions
- **Real-time Streaming:** Moving from daily aggregates to real-time intraday news processing.
- **Fine-tuning:** Fine-tuning a smaller model (e.g., Llama 3) specifically on financial news to reduce costs and dependency on external APIs.
- **Graph RAG:** Implementing Graph Retrieval-Augmented Generation to better understand the relationships between entities (e.g., Companies, Politicians) mentioned in the news.
- **Multimodal Analysis:** Experimenting with multimodal LLMs to directly ingest chart images (candlestick patterns) alongside textual news, allowing the model to "see" technical formations like a human trader.
